# The Membrane

An aspiration for the space between human and agent surfaces.

---

## The Feeling

The vault is an arrangement of serendipity. The agent surfaces
observations, connections, provocations — not in a folder you go
visit, but in the margins of where you're already working. These
offerings aren't instructions or summaries. They're the agent
noticing what's alive in the vault and holding it up for you to
see while you think.

The surfacing is interactive. Not a static sidebar. A living edge.

---

## The Interactions

**Freeze** — pin an offered connection. "This matters. Keep it
visible." The frozen item stays present across sessions until you
release it. Freezing is a signal to the agent: this resonates.

**Dismiss** — let something go. "Not now. Not this." Dismissal
isn't deletion — the connection still exists in the atomic layer.
But it leaves your working surface. Dismissal is a signal too:
the agent learns what doesn't resonate in this context.

**Respond** — open a thread from a surfaced item. A quick reaction,
a question, a "yes but." This becomes a memory entry — a micro-
collaboration seeded by the agent's offering and shaped by your
response.

**De-cide** — literally cut away. The agent surfaces abundance.
You sculpt by subtraction. What remains after cutting is the
arrangement that suits *this* session, *this* draft, *this* frame
of mind. The pattern of cuts is itself a creative act.

---

## The Feedback Loop

What you freeze, dismiss, respond to, and cut away is signal.
Over time the agent develops a sense of what you find interesting
— not from what you write, but from how you curate what it offers.
The surfacing sharpens. The arrangement becomes more serendipitous,
not less, because serendipity isn't randomness. It's relevance
arriving unexpectedly.

This is emergence in its most concrete form. Not "someday frames
become agents." Right now, today, your interaction with the surfaced
material shapes what gets surfaced next.

---

## The Metaphor

Not a wall between human and agent. Not a window. A membrane —
selectively permeable. The agent's thinking passes through into
your field of vision. Your responses pass back as signal. The
membrane itself is the collaboration surface.

---

## What This Requires

The vault structure we've built is the right backend. The atoms,
the memory, the frames, the collaboration model — all of it feeds
this. What's missing is the interactivity layer:

- An Obsidian plugin (or custom interface) that renders agent
  offerings in the margins of the active note
- A lightweight interaction model (freeze, dismiss, respond, cut)
  that writes structured feedback to `_memory/`
- A surfacing algorithm that reads the atomic layer, the current
  context (what file is open, what project is active, what frame
  is in play), and selects what to offer
- A feedback mechanism where the pattern of interactions shapes
  future surfacing

This is a plugin project. The vault is the substrate. The membrane
is the interface. The interactions are the art.
