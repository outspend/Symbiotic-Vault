# The Compost

An experiment in reading what was neglected, abandoned, or passed
over — and asking what the pattern of neglect reveals.

---

## The Insight

Abraham Wald, working for the Statistical Research Group in WWII,
was asked to recommend where to add armor to bombers. The military
showed him where returning planes had bullet holes. Wald said:
armor the places with *no* holes. Those are the spots where planes
that got hit didn't come back.

The vault has its own survivorship bias. The atoms that get tended,
the fragments that become drafts, the connections that get traced —
these are the returning planes. But what about the stream entries
that never got atomized? The seed atoms that never developed? The
draft fragments that got abandoned? The inbox items that sat
unprocessed?

The neglected material isn't failure. It's signal. Read as a body,
it describes the shape of your blind spots — the ideas you circled
but didn't commit to, the connections you sensed but didn't follow,
the threads you dropped without noticing.

---

## The Experiment

### Step 1: Gather the Neglected

The agent surveys the vault for material that was passed over:
- Stream entries with no atoms sourced from them
- Atoms stuck at `status: seed` beyond a threshold (e.g., 30 days)
- Inbox items never processed
- Draft fragments in projects that stalled or were abandoned
- Atoms with zero inbound links (no one references them)

### Step 2: Read the Absence

The agent reads this body of neglected material not as a list of
things to fix, but as a *text*. What is it about, taken together?
What themes recur in the abandoned? What kind of thinking gets
started but not finished? Is there a coherent concern hiding in the
pattern of what was dropped?

This is the counter-intuitive analysis: don't ask "what should I
have done with these?" Ask "what does it mean that I didn't?"

### Step 3: The Report

Write to `_memory/` a reflection structured as:

**The Neglected Body:** What material was found and how much.

**The Pattern:** What the abandoned material has in common, read
as its own coherent text. Themes, tones, recurring concerns.

**The Wald Question:** Where are the holes that *aren't* there?
What does the shape of neglect suggest about what you're avoiding,
what makes you uncomfortable, or what you don't yet have a frame
for?

**The Compost:** What might grow from this material if it were
tended? Not a to-do list — a speculative reading of what the
neglected layer could become if given attention. Sometimes the
richest growth comes from decay.

---

## What This Reveals About the Vault

The compost experiment tests whether provenance works in both
directions. The vault tracks how ideas develop *forward* — random
to rough to refined. But the compost reads the vault *backward* —
what didn't develop, and why. If the trail is the treasure, then
the places where the trail goes cold are treasure too.

Over time, running the compost periodically produces its own
archive in `_memory/` — a longitudinal record of your blind spots.
Do the same themes keep appearing in the neglected layer? That's
a pattern worth naming. Does something finally leave the compost
and start developing? That's a resurrection story with full
provenance: "this idea was abandoned on this date, composted on
this date, noticed again on this date, and finally atomized."

---

## Connection to Frames

The compost might be where new frames are born. If the neglected
material has coherence that no existing frame captures, that's
the strongest possible signal for a proposed frame — a perspective
the vault needs but doesn't have yet. Tend proposes frames from
density in the atomic layer. The compost proposes frames from
*absence*.
