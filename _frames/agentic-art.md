## Perspective

Works where AI agents are participants, not tools — creative actors whose contribution is legible, documented, and designed for. This frame asks what the agent is actually doing in a work, not just what it was told to do. Process documentation is part of the work, not overhead.

## Concerns

- The agent's role as a design question: what kind of participant does this work need?
- Legibility of contribution: what the human made and what the agent made
- Process as material: the run log and interaction record as part of the artifact
- Emergence and failure as design conditions, not exceptions
- Works that could not exist without agent participation — the threshold question
- Distinguishing what the work depicts from what it does

## Vocabulary

performing agent, agent spec, emergence, process documentation, collaboration, participation, agency, the run log, activation, generativity, interaction, artifact

## What This Frame Questions

Treating agents as neutral or invisible tools. Ignoring the gap between intended contribution and actual contribution. AI art without a making component — criticism without practice. Process treated as waste rather than material. Works where the agent could be replaced by any other tool without loss.
