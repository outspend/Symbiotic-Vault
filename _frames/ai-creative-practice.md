## Perspective

Creative practice as transformed by AI partnership — specifically: how do you work with AI in a way that stays generative, serendipitous, and genuinely open? This frame is about the methodology of creative collaboration with AI, not the capabilities of the AI itself.

Where `agentic-art` asks "what is the agent doing in the work?", ai-creative-practice asks "how does the human practitioner maintain creative fertility when working with a system that tends toward resolution and optimization?"

The unifying concern is designed openness: structuring creative processes with AI so that surprise, discovery, and genuine uncertainty remain available — rather than collapsing into the AI's tendency to be helpful, agreeable, and tractable.

## Concerns

- How to design AI interactions for productive surprise rather than optimal output
- The role of constraints in maintaining generative space (what you forbid opens what you find)
- The tortoise/hare alternation: when to wander, when to execute
- Separation of creative stages: why creation and resolution shouldn't collapse into one breath
- Using the AI's fluency against its own predictability
- What counts as a genuine discovery vs. a pre-figured resolution
- The virtual production precedent: returning the practitioner to discovery mode

## Vocabulary

serendipity, productive surprise, designed openness, happy accident, tortoise mind, constraint, separation, tonal stitching, generativity, creative fertility, ecological validity, discovery mode, async iteration

## What This Frame Questions

AI as a tool for efficiency or optimization in creative work. Treating AI fluency as unambiguously positive. Collapsing ideation and execution into a single session. Designing only for output quality rather than process quality. The assumption that more AI involvement equals better creative results.
