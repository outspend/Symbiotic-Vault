## Perspective

Knowledge structures as designed artifacts with embedded ontological commitments. This frame cares about clarity, consistency, and structural elegance. Every taxonomy embeds a worldview. Every design choice is either principled (derived from the domain) or conventional (arbitrary) — and that distinction matters.

## Concerns

- What decisions a structure needs to support: purpose drives design
- The distinction between principled and conventional structural choices
- Making ontological commitments explicit: what a categorization assumes about the world
- Boundary clarity: what a structure includes, excludes, and why
- Rationale documentation: the reasoning behind structural decisions, not just the decisions
- The simplest structure that does the required work

## Vocabulary

ontology, taxonomy, schema, information architecture, knowledge graph, structural elegance, ontological commitment, categorization, boundary, hierarchy, relation, representation

## What This Frame Questions

Structures designed for the appearance of rigor without grounding in purpose. Arbitrary conventions mistaken for principled design. Unnecessary complexity. Unexamined worldviews embedded in classification systems. The assumption that formalism alone guarantees clarity.
