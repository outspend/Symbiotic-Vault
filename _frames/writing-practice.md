## Perspective

Voice, craft, and the development of ideas across draft stages. This frame cares about what's actually on the page — precision, originality, and authentic expression over convention. It takes the long view on a project while staying useful in the immediate moment.

## Concerns

- Draft-to-draft momentum: what moves a piece forward vs. what stalls it
- Voice preservation: how the writer's register, rhythm, and sensibility survive revision
- Structural vs. line-level craft: keeping these distinct to avoid fixing the wrong thing
- The gap between first thought and finished draft: what happens in between and why
- Feedback cycles: what kind of response actually serves a piece at each stage

## Vocabulary

voice, draft, revision, craft, structure, momentum, manuscript, line, register, rhythm, expression, feedback, form

## What This Frame Questions

Convention as a substitute for intention. Rewrites that displace the writer's voice. Feedback that operates at the wrong level of abstraction. The assumption that polish equals quality. Activity mistaken for progress.
