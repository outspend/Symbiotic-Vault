### What the phone transcript holds that the comic hasn't used

These are the unused beats from `_journal/2026-04-22.md` that would let you make it text:

1. **Line 34 — Sadie / Susan Atkins on the hanging bed.**
   *"he would have a chance to fool around with the girls during the day — sadie glutz (susan denise atkins — one of the killers) (on a hanging bed, felt very public but weird to him he didnt want to do it again)"*
   This is the missing seduction beat. Mike *had sex with a future killer*, on a hanging bed, **publicly**, and **recoiled**. "Felt public" is the key — Charlie was the audience. The honey was already being served to Mike; he just didn't fully bite. This is the thing that, when shown, makes "under what circumstances?" land as a man who has already privately refused once.

2. **Line 56 — the LeBrun bus offer.**
   *"a warped offer of a girl for their son/child. that was charlie again."*
   Mike's own memory contains a documented instance of Charlie pulling up in a *black bus* offering a girl as recruitment currency. The honey trap as Charlie's actual operating method, in Mike's voice, with the same black-bus iconography as P4. Not in the comic at all.

3. **Line 38 — "Charlie was about not owning things… no personal ownership — giving things away."**
   The script gestures at this via the jacket, but the philosophy itself isn't named. It's the contract Mike is half-signing. The progression scales cleanly: *don't own your jacket → don't own your body → don't own your life.* That ladder is in the transcript and the script doesn't climb it.

4. **Line 65 — the Chaos book frame.**
   *"this story is in response to the 'chaos' charlie manson book i recommended he read."*
   The actual reason Mike is telling this story today. The current ending uses Bugliosi (which Mike refused). Chaos is the trigger he accepted. Different, and missing.

The transcript doesn't quite support sexual jealousy in the pedestrian sense — it supports something colder and more useful: Charlie offering Mike the deal (girls, no property, music, warmth) and pricing the partial yes. The look across the panel border on the new P3 wants to be **the recruiter clocking the holdout**, not a jilted lover. That makes "would you die for me?" a closer, not a confrontation — Charlie's been laying the trap since the first jacket-on.
