# SUMMER WITH CHARLIE
### A comic script — work in progress


---

## PAGE ONE 

**PANEL 1** — *Title spread, top-left*
Lush night foliage, flowers. Stylized retro lettering.

> **TITLE:** Summer with Charlie

---

**PANEL 2** — *Top-right. Night. MIKE crouches at the edge of a creek, looking across at distant firelight.*

> **CAPTION (Mike, VO):** I was living off of Highvale Trail in Topanga Canyon. One night I saw lights across the creek.

---

**PANEL 3** — *Bottom-left. Mike moving through dark brush and foliage, half-crouching.*

> **CAPTION:** Following sounds in the dark. Same as when I followed my dog. That always ended in poison oak.

---

**PANEL 4** — *Bottom-right. Wide shot. A large commune gathering around a campfire — string lights, art, half-nude figures.*

> **CAPTION:** It was a really great scene.

---

## PAGE TWO

**PANEL 1** — *Top, wide. Interior communal room. Charlie Manson seated center with a guitar, mostly women arranged around him in a circle.*

> **CAPTION:** They shared everything.  


---

**PANEL 2** — *Bottom-left. Close on Charlie — bearded, relaxed, fingers on guitar strings. Looking page right towards panel 3.*

> **CAPTION:** Charlie played guitar. Depression-era 40s folk style. His way of tuning the room.

---

**PANEL 3** — *Bottom-right. Mike sits next to SADIE, suede jacket still on, bashful and engrossed.*

> **CAPTION:** I kept my suede jacket on. I didn't hang it up. That way I didn't have to give it away.  I really liked that jacket.

---

## PAGE THREE 

**PANEL 1** — *Top-left. Daytime. Sadie leading Mike by the hand into the commune — Manson family members painting, working outdoors near their building.*

> **CAPTION:** I started coming by during the day.

---

**PANEL 2** — *Top-right. Interior. A hanging canopy bed, two silhouettes visible behind the fabric — intimate but exposed. Other women observe from the edges.*

> **CAPTION:** I remember a hanging bed with Sadie. Very public. 
> **CAPTION:** Something felt slightly off.

---

**PANEL 3** — *Bottom-left. Magic hour. Mike walking alone on a Topanga trail. A large spider web in the foreground.*

> **CAPTION:** Every night I went back to my spot on Highvale.  My jacket stayed with me.

---

**PANEL 4** — *Bottom-right. Night. Full moon. Charlie surrounded by his family around a fire.*

> **CAPTION:** And every night, Charlie wove.

---

## PAGE FOUR 

**PANEL 1** — *Top. Exterior commune, day. Billy arrives carrying a wooden yoke with four large jars of honey hanging from it. Mike stands beside him as the girls greet them.*

> **CAPTION:** I met my buddy Billy at a commune - Evan's place on Old Topanga Canyon Road.
> **CAPTION:** We brought four gallons of his honey to help them along.

---

**PANEL 2** — *Bottom-left. Charlie at a car, handing the keys to an older woman — the landlady. Honey jars arrayed between them. Second caption over the honey jars*

> **CAPTION:** Turns out they were crashing the place. Chuck sweet-talked the landlady — gave her a car.
> **CAPTION:** He'd do what he had to to keep the thing going.

---

**PANEL 3** — *Bottom-right. Topanga Canyon at sunset. Two figures — Mike and Billy — shading their eyes at the sunset.   squinting into the light. taking in the warmth.*

> **CAPTION:** We were all trying something. Sometimes you have to get out into the open before things get interesting.  
> **CAPTION:** That's where the poison oak is too.

---

## PAGE FIVE 

**PANEL 1** — *Top, wide. Night. Charlie and Mike stand facing each other in the headlights of the commune bus. The campfire glows far behind them.*

> **CAPTION:** Eventually Charlie got around to me. 

---

**PANEL 2** — *Middle-left. Close on Charlie's face — dark eyes, still.*

> **CHARLIE (SPEECH BUBBLE):** Would you die for me?

---

**PANEL 3** — *Middle-right. Low angle. Just their feet and legs in the pooled headlight, the tension held in stillness.*

> **CAPTION:** I asked him "under what circumstances?"

---

**PANEL 4** — *Bottom, wide. Charlie walks away, back towards the group/ the commune fire. Mike stands alone at the bus.*

> **CAPTION:** Wrong answer. 
> **CAPTION:** I remember him quoting Nietzsche. I'm sure he had it exactly right.

---

## PAGE SIX 

**PANEL 1** — *Top-left. Topanga Canyon in daylight — hills, the ocean visible far below. Quiet.*

> **CAPTION:** I had a feeling there'd be trouble if I came around again.

---

**PANEL 2** — *Top-right. Two hands hold a Los Angeles Times (picturing charlie and Sadie/Susan). The headline reads:*

> **NEWSPAPER HEADLINE:** TATE-LA BIANCA MURDERS SHOCK CITY

> **CAPTION:** When the murders happened and everything was public — I didn't want to read the Bugliosi book.

---

**PANEL 3** — *Bottom, wide. Sunset over Topanga Creek. Golden light on the canyon. The scene that opened the comic, now stilled.*

> **CAPTION (left):** It was a really great scene.
> **CAPTION (right):** And then it wasn't.
