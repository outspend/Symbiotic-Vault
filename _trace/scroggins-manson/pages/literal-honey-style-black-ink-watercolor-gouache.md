# Literal Honey — style-transform prompt (black ink + watercolor/gouache)

**Experimental.** Tests a mixed-media style transform: heavy posterized black ink carries the drawing, while watercolor and gouache handle color, light, and form. This preserves the finished page's layout, captions, characters, staging, and beats while changing the rendering language.

**To use:** paste from `## Render prompt` through to the end of the file into ChatGPT and attach the finished page as the reference image.

---

## Render prompt

> Render the attached comic page with posterized black-ink treatment for the drawing and watercolor/gouache for the color and form rendering. The attached image is the source of truth for layout, panel arrangement, character positions, staging, beats, eyelines, captions (exact text and position), and object counts. Change only the rendering style.
>
> **Style — posterized black ink plus watercolor/gouache color:**
>
> - Keep heavy black ink shapes as the dominant drawing layer
> - Posterized black shadows, bold contour lines, and large graphic ink masses
> - Use watercolor and gouache washes for color, light, and form beneath and around the black ink
> - Opaque gouache passages for clothing, honey jars, porch wood, car, and key objects
> - Transparent watercolor blooms and granulation in sky, foliage, dust, canyon road, and sunset
> - Visible brush texture, pigment pooling, dry-brush edges, and soft wash edges
> - Forms should be rendered with painterly watercolor/gouache color fields, but held together by strong black ink design
> - Limited fine hatching; rely on black shape, gouache opacity, and watercolor washes instead
> - Handmade mixed-media page: black ink plate over hand-painted watercolor/gouache, not polished digital painting
>
> **Preserve exactly from the attached image:**
>
> - Page shape: 3:2 landscape, exactly 3 panels (1 full-width top + 2 bottom)
> - Panel borders, gutter positions, relative panel sizes
> - Caption boxes: positions, exact text, slightly yellow tone
> - Character positions, staging, eyelines, beats, and object counts
> - Same environment across all three panels (Topanga Canyon commune, day → sunset arc)
>
> **Protected readability:**
>
> - Caption boxes remain pale yellow with clean black readable text
> - Panel 3 figures remain readable against the sunset, with Billy left and Mike right
> - Billy, Mike, Chuck, Landlady, and the commune women remain identifiable
> - Honey jars remain identifiable in Panel 1 and Panel 2
> - The key handoff remains the visual center of Panel 2
>
> **Character continuity:**
>
> - Billy remains tall with long curly blond hair, full beard, open fringed leather vest, necklaces, denim cutoffs, and cowboy boots
> - Mike remains clean-shaven with dark shaggy hair, brown suede jacket, cream t-shirt, jeans, and boots
> - Chuck remains intense, magnetic, Manson-like, with shoulder-length dark hair, trimmed beard, white shirt, and dark pants
> - Landlady remains stern, older, gray hair in bun, purple floral dress
> - Commune women remain distinct adult hippie women in patchwork dresses
>
> **Color and rendering notes:**
>
> - Keep colors richer and more tactile than flat silkscreen, but avoid realistic photographic rendering
> - Let watercolor/gouache create form through loose patches, washes, opacity shifts, and brush pressure
> - Preserve the sunset warmth in Panel 3 with watercolor blooms and gouache silhouettes
> - Let black ink carry structure in faces, hands, hair, foliage, porch, car, and road
>
> **DO NOT:**
>
> - Do not redesign the layout or change the panel arrangement
> - Do not lose, paraphrase, or move captions
> - Do not add unnamed characters
> - Do not add motion lines, sparkles, exclamation effects, or blur unless the source has them
> - Do not add sidebars, keys, panel numbers, or production-sheet elements
> - Do not make the page abstract or illegible
> - Do not turn it into a single poster without comic panels
> - Do not replace the black-ink structure with soft watercolor-only rendering
> - Do not use clean vector art or glossy digital-paint finish
>
> **Final instruction**
>
> Output only the finished comic page in posterized black ink with watercolor/gouache coloring and form rendering, with all other elements preserved from the attached image.
