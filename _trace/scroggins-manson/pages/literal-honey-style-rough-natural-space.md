# Literal Honey — style-transform prompt (rough natural space)

**Experimental.** This prompt preserves the finished page's characters, captions, panel structure, and readable story objects while rebuilding the natural space through loose, quick, rough color-shape construction. It is tuned toward the successful sky/foliage/earth behavior from the strongest bright landscape pass, without relying on a named artist or a fixed color palette.

**To use:** paste from `## Render prompt` through to the end of the file into ChatGPT and attach the finished page as the reference image.

---

## Render prompt

> Keep the characters, captions, and story objects readable, but rebuild the natural space with loose, quick, rough color-shape construction. The important change is the handling of sky, earth, hills, road surface, scrub, trees, and foliage. The attached image is the source of truth for layout, panel arrangement, character positions, staging, beats, eyelines, captions (exact text and position), and object counts.
>
> **Selective rule:**
>
> - Preserve the characters mostly in their current American pulp comic illustration style: faces, anatomy, poses, clothing identities, expressions, and relationships stay recognizable.
> - Preserve captions, panel borders, honey jars, yoke, keys, table, school bus, blue car, porch/house, telephone pole, wires, easel, chair, pots, and hanging plant as readable story objects.
> - Transform the natural space: sky, clouds, earth, hills, canyon road surface, scrub, trees, foliage masses, sunset, distant ridges, and ground planes.
>
> **Natural-space treatment:**
>
> - Redesign the natural space as loose, quick, rough color-shape construction.
> - Use stacked patches of color for trees and foliage, reducing leaves and branches into layered irregular masses.
> - Let foliage read as overlapping colored shapes, not detailed plants.
> - Treat sky as fast abbreviated marks and broken color bands, not realistic clouds.
> - Use rough directional strokes, uneven painted patches, quick gestural color placement, and irregular handmade transitions.
> - Keep the natural world lively, unfinished, and rhythmically simplified.
> - Earth, road, and hills should become patterned planes of broken color.
> - Preserve the approximate amount and placement of natural forms, but rebuild them through layered rough color shapes.
> - The sky and natural space should feel like they were laid in fast and confidently with abbreviated marks, not slowly rendered.
> - In Panel 1, let the blue sky and background vegetation become quick directional marks and stacked green/blue/ochre/pink patches.
> - In Panel 2, let the hills, trees, and open air behind the car and figures become layered broken-color planes.
> - In Panel 3, make the sunset sky a field of rough bands, fast marks, and broken warm/cool color shapes, with road and hills rebuilt as patterned planes.
>
> **Protected characters and key story objects:**
>
> - Billy remains tall with long curly blond hair, full beard, open fringed leather vest, necklaces, denim cutoffs, and cowboy boots.
> - Mike remains clean-shaven with dark shaggy hair, brown suede jacket, cream t-shirt, jeans, and boots.
> - Chuck remains intense, magnetic, Manson-like, with shoulder-length dark hair, trimmed beard, white shirt, and dark pants.
> - Landlady remains stern, older, gray hair in bun, purple floral dress.
> - Commune women remain distinct adult hippie women in patchwork dresses.
> - Honey jars remain golden amber.
> - The school bus remains a dark painted school bus in Panel 1.
> - The blue car remains recognizably blue in Panel 2.
> - The porch/house remains readable as a weathered wooden structure.
>
> **Preserve exactly from the attached image:**
>
> - Page shape: 3:2 landscape, exactly 3 panels (1 full-width top + 2 bottom)
> - Panel borders, gutter positions, relative panel sizes
> - Caption boxes: positions, exact text, slightly yellow tone
> - Character positions, staging, eyelines, beats, and object counts
> - Same story locations and day-to-sunset arc
>
> **Caption handling:**
>
> - Preserve the caption boxes and exact words as much as possible.
> - Keep captions legible, black lettering on slightly yellow boxes.
> - Do not replace caption lettering with decorative typography.
>
> **Avoid:**
>
> - neat illustrative clouds
> - smooth atmospheric skies
> - detailed botanical foliage
> - polished landscape painting
> - clean vector-like simplification
> - over-rendered realism
> - removing or minimizing the natural-space treatment
> - making the whole page abstract or illegible
> - changing the panel layout
> - adding characters, sidebars, keys, panel numbers, motion lines, sparkles, or exclamation effects
>
> **Final instruction**
>
> Output only the finished comic page: pulp-style characters and readable story objects preserved, natural space rebuilt as loose, quick, rough layered color-shape construction.
