Using an LLM to stitch disparate tonal inputs — notecards with scenes, vibes, or ideas — into a single coherent work with a through-line. The method takes advantage of the LLM's fluency across registers and its capacity to find unexpected coherence between strikingly different inputs.

The proposed experiment: collect a set of scene notecards (disparate tones, potentially incompatible), arrange three, then ask the agent to generate the transitional through-line — or simply stitch and transition between them to make a single work. The quality of the result would vary with the degree of tonal difference and the agent's fluency in navigating it.

A key design constraint: the agent that creates the scenes should not be the same agent that stitches them, or the stitching and resolution emerge from the same breath and feel pre-determined. The generativity depends on real distance between the scene-creation and stitching steps.
