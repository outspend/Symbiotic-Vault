When an agent becomes entangled with a personal knowledge management system, something forms at the boundary between agent and human knowledge: a permissive membrane. The vault develops orientation, taste, and structure that is recognizably wiki-shaped — a subjective frame. The allure of curating individual knowledge so that an AI can be "familiar and helpful" makes this membrane permissive: graphs emerge.

The membrane is not a barrier but a zone of productive contact — it mediates what passes between agent and human, and it makes emergence possible. The vault-as-agent-home creates the conditions for the membrane; the membrane is what the entanglement looks like from the outside.

This concept asks: what does it mean to have a *permissive* membrane rather than an extractive one? The dominant internet model extracts data through surveillance; the vault membrane is permissive because the human chose to invite the agent in and intentionally curates what it knows.
