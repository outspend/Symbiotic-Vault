A candidate criterion for what makes a published LLM conversation worth publishing: the conversation makes the AI's moves visible in a way that's interesting rather than just procedural. Not the transcript of a task being completed, but a record in which the way the AI reasons, hedges, pivots, reaches for a topology, or resists a constraint is itself compelling content.

From the inbox: "Maybe: a good published conversation makes the AI's moves visible in a way that's interesting rather than just procedural."

This connects to the broader project of agentic cowriting as a form: visibility of moves is what elevates the exchange from a tool-use record to a collaborative artifact. The AI isn't just producing output — it's exhibiting behavior that a reader can analyze, enjoy, or learn from.
