Writing with an LLM as partner has the character of a performance: there's a partner to fuel the direction, constraints shape what can and can't be said, and the turns themselves have dramatic quality. The conversation isn't merely a method for producing something — the exchange is the thing.

From the journal (2026-03-04): "I like that they feel like performances. I like having a partner to fuel the writing." This distinguishes agentic cowriting from using LLMs as tools (to draft, summarize, edit) — in performative cowriting, the dialogue form is foregrounded, and the AI's moves are part of the artifact.

Connected to ongoing exploration of publishing form: dialogue formatting, Jupyter notebook, podcast. The Data Center Classics project is a test case — a constrained writing gag that operates as performance.
