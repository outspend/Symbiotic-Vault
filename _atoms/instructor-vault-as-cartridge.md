An instructor's vault could function as a curriculum cartridge — a self-contained educational package that student vaults dock with or reference. The student vault is the personal creative/learning space; the instructor vault provides the structured course context, assignments, and progression. The two vaults communicate: the student's agent has access to the instructor's material, and the instructor's agent can observe or summarize patterns across student vaults.

The design vision includes Spotify-style end-of-class summaries for the instructor — trending concepts, spotlight student comments or projects, pattern recognition across cohorts. Student agentic experiments could be voted on weekly, rewarding those whose agents engaged most richly with the material.

This extends the cartridge metaphor (from `llm-conversation-as-cartridge`) to an educational context: the cartridge is not a conversation but a course, and docking is the student bringing their own context to meet structured content.
