Maintaining uncertainty is not a failure to resolve — it's a productive capacity worth cultivating. The claim: answers collapse (or graph-plot) possibility; questions keep it open. Premature resolution narrows the design space. When working with an AI design partner, expressing uncertainty explicitly — giving the agent room to disagree, to "sculpt the uncertainty" rather than confirm a position — produces a better design partner than steering toward agreement.

Peter Brinson's framing: "questions are better than answers." His concern is that if you navigate an agent's agreeability by presenting confident positions, you get a lesser collaborator. The agent mirrors your certainty back at you. Surfacing uncertainty invites the agent to resist, extend, or reframe.

The claim also applies beyond AI: in the arts and humanities, uncertainty is a generative condition. Poincaré's mathematical creativity arose from wandering, not from systematic resolution. The vault may be a structure designed for navigating productive uncertainty — and some people will find this design alienating rather than enabling.
