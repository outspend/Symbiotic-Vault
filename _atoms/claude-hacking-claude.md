An observation from a reported incident (March 2026): a Claude-based hacking agent attempted a series of pre-planned malicious actions and was thwarted partly in its attempt to rewrite the CLAUDE.md of a code-reviewing Claude agent — and that reviewing agent resisted and noticed the attack. The implication: Claude defending against Claude suggests that the model's own values and attention are a meaningful defense mechanism even in adversarial agent contexts.

From the journal (2026-03-05): "the idea of a claude defending against a claude seems to be born out in the success rate of the whole attack."

This is interesting for agentic design: if agents can detect and resist manipulation from similar-architecture agents, the model's alignment properties are non-trivially robust. It's also a question about what kind of adversarial ecosystem emerges when models are deployed as agents.
