A vault that is not only a home but a deployable agent — prepopulated with context and sent out to do work in the world, then routed back to the home vault's inbox. The user's concrete image: sending a prepopulated vault to interview someone (e.g., Bernt Wahl). The vault would be integrated with an agent and seeded with a project; it would encourage journaling in the subject, harvest what it needs, and return the material.

The design principle: the vault knows just what aspects of itself are pertinent to a given project or collaboration. It shares selectively. It can be scheduled to urge journaling or capture, taking friction out of the subject's contribution.

There may be a simpler form: the home vault's agent sends a task out, monitors it, and routes the result to inbox. The deployable vault is the more ambitious version — a full context sent as a diplomatic package.
