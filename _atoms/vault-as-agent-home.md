The Obsidian vault is not merely a human knowledge tool that an agent assists with — it is, structurally, a natural habitat for agents. The reliance on markdown, the simplicity and flexibility, the ease of extension: the whole thing is built for nesting. The vault is to Claude what an abandoned snail shell is to a hermit crab.

This reframe matters because it changes the question from "how can an AI help me with my notes?" to "what kind of agent grows in a vault like this?" Once any agent is allowed in and becomes entangled with the PKM system, it begins writing its memory there, establishing persistence, developing orientation and even taste. The vault becomes a subjective frame with a structure that is recognizably wiki.

Peter Brinson (feedback, 2026-03-17) noted that the hermit crab / snail shell analogy is the key structural metaphor in the user's writing — it should win out if competing analogies arise.
