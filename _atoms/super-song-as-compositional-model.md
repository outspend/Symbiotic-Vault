A compositional model in which a single work is made of distinct soundscapes or tonal movements — lesser songs that could have been expanded into three separate pieces but are instead stitched into one. The result feels epic or narrative rather than simple. "Band on the Run" (Wings) is the user's primary example; "A Day in the Life" (Beatles) is another.

The shadow of the super-song: short, repetitive songs that feel like empty calories — the same tonal material held without development. The super-song is distinguished by its navigation of transition: how you move between strikingly different registers while maintaining coherence.

Applied to writing: a work could be structured as a collection of notecards with scenes on them — disparate tones or vibes, arranged into a through-line by a transitional engine. The challenge is the stitching: what provides coherence across tonal difference?
