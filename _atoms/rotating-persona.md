A prompting method for encouraging plurality of voice and perspective: invite the LLM to adopt rotating personas (chimera, protean — figures of multitude and transformation), asking when, why, and who speaks for any given response. Rather than fixing the model to a single voice or role, the method lets different perspectives surface and negotiates which voice is responding.

From LLM Best Practices: "rotating persona (chimera, protean). Encourage a multitude. The question becomes who and when and why."

The chimera reference is deliberate — a figure composed of incompatible parts, made coherent by proximity rather than unity. The protean reference emphasizes transformation: the persona shifts by necessity or invitation, not as deception but as range.
