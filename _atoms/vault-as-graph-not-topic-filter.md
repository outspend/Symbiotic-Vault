A structuring principle for vaults and knowledge systems: organize as a graph (relational, edge-defined, traversable) rather than as a topic filter (categorical, hierarchical, folder-defined). What you gain: connections become first-class citizens; emergent paths and clusters become visible; agents and tools can navigate the structure as a network rather than a taxonomy.

Sparked by a Reddit post on an MCP server that treats an Obsidian vault as a graph — prompting questions about what's gained by that approach, whether it suggests modifications to vault structure, and what a knowledge graph approach enables that topic-based filing doesn't.

The question of whether to modify the vault structure for agents remains open: if agents navigate the graph, the topology of the graph is the interface.
