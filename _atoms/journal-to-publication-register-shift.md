The shift from journal writing to publishable artifact requires a deliberate change of register. In the journal, the mode is racing — getting things captured, getting shit out. In publication, the mode is inhabiting: slowing down, elaborating, living inside the thinking long enough for readers to follow.

Peter Brinson's feedback on the user's 2026-03-14 journal entry made this explicit: fleshing out context rather than racing through, explaining rather than referencing. The journal is for the writer; the publication is for the reader. The editorial move is learning to sense which register you're in and when to shift.

Related strategies: linking out to explanatory articles (without interrupting the reader's flow), choosing consistent terminology rather than synonym-switching, repeating key terms more than feels natural in conversation.
