An open question about form and curation: what distinguishes an LLM conversation worth publishing from noise? The question was prompted by a March 2026 meeting that surfaced Jupyter notebook publishing to a shared wiki as a viable format — technically plausible, but the curatorial criteria remain unanswered. The vocabulary for the object itself is unsettled: repo, deliverable, knowledge unit.

A working hypothesis: the exchange has to do work that a conventional essay or document wouldn't. The back-and-forth needs to be the point, not merely the method. A good published conversation makes the AI's moves visible in a way that's interesting rather than just procedural.

Related considerations: what makes a *human* conversation worth publishing (interviews, Socratic dialogues, letters)? What transfers? And can a published conversation be a "cartridge" for other media — song, image, video?
