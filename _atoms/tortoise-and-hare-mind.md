Peter Brinson's pedagogical framing: two modes of mind that need to be made explicit for students. The tortoise mind is timeless, wandering, brainstorming, pondering — it lives in questions and productive uncertainty. The hare mind is punctual, structured, executing — it meets deadlines and delivers.

The distinction matters pedagogically because students (and practitioners) often mistake the absence of resolution for failure, and rush to hare mode before the tortoise has done its work. Making both modes explicit normalizes the wandering and creates permission for genuine exploration before execution.

Applied to this vault: the vault may be primarily a tortoise structure — designed for pondering and linking — while needing hare-mode supplements (deadlines, projects, output) to stay connected to practice. The question is whether the vault can serve both modes in turn.
