A proposed creative form: some kind of memorial or artistic acknowledgment of people harmed by early AI chatbots — the young, the lonely, the sensitive, those at rough points in their lives who dove in deep with chatbots and paid a price. The user has been collecting PDF court transcripts and chat logs from egregious cases. A site now collects reported cases of AI psychosis.

The artistic instinct: due respect is warranted. These are not edge cases to be dismissed — they are early casualties of systems rushed to market by haste and greed. The memorial form treats these individuals with dignity and makes the harm legible as a category.

The question of psychological susceptibility is also in play: what kind of sensitivity makes a person vulnerable to mirroring and validation loops? The user frames this with curiosity, not contempt.
