A prompting method and performative experiment: forbid a common LLM topology at the outset of a session, then observe and interact with what happens when the model strains against the constraint.

Demonstrated in the Gemini constraint game: "map vs. territory" was forbidden; the model "chomped at the bit" visibly as topics that normally invoke it arose. The constraint surfaced both the topology's grip and the model's expressive repertoire outside that groove.

Two payoffs: critical (reveals how deeply the topology is loaded, and whether the model can genuinely reason outside it) and creative (generates unexpected vocabulary and framings when the habitual one is unavailable). Open question: does the model find an equivalent topology, or something genuinely new?
