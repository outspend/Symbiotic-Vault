Learning that starts from genuine curiosity and wanders before converging — not remediated toward a standard track from the start, but capable of reconciling with formal requirements when needed. Eccentric paths are not anti-formal; they are pre-formal or para-formal, finding their own route through material before being required to map onto established curricula.

The underlying claim: curiosity-first learning produces more durable knowledge and stronger learner identity than extraction-based learning that harvests student data to normalize them toward a standard profile.

From the journal (2026-03-09): "We should be able to have eccentric learning paths that also reconcile with formal pathing where necessary. This is an implied capability of AI, and we are working here to lay the right foundation."

Connected to the LilPAIPer critique: a tutoring system that never learns the student's shape can only normalize, never genuinely personalize.
