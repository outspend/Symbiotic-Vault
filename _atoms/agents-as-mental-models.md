Agentic systems are externalized versions of the mental models that humans carry of each other. Before we had agents, humans simulated mental models all the time — a CEO's team filtered decisions to only let the hardest ones reach the top (Obama's version); a factory let workers model the boss and answer as him (Warhol's version). Agentics are mental models made explicit, portable, and operable.

Peter Brinson framed this as: "Before we had agents, mental models existed in humans. Simulating mental models." The agents don't introduce something new — they make visible what was always happening in human systems of representation, delegation, and impersonation.

This reframing has implications for how AI agents are designed and evaluated: the question is not "how accurate is the model?" but "what kind of mental model is this, and who benefits from its simulation?"
