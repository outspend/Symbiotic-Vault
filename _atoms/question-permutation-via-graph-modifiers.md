A teaching and prompting method: treat a question as a graph node connected to separate modifier nodes (frames, contexts, constraints, perspectives). Permuting the modifiers — swapping or adding frame nodes — produces reframings of the original question. Students "roll" a question by selecting different combinations of modifiers.

Sparked by a canvas-like UI for AI conversations (Reddit), with interest in whether it fits in JupyterLab/wiki3.ai. The key innovation: separating the question from its modifiers as discrete graph elements allows systematic reframing rather than ad hoc rephrasing. This becomes a teachable operation.

From the inbox: "Teach students to permute and roll a question via a question and separated graph modifiers of the question and the frame!"
