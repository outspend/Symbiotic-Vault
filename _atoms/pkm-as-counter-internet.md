Personal knowledge management, and especially its agentic turn, suggests an earlier internet — one organized around intentional ideation rather than engagement optimization. When the web became a marketplace for attention merchants, what was cultivated was the lowest common denominator: engagement über alles. The surveillance profiles pulled from market internet are potentially feral; the profiles that emerge from personal knowledge management are curated, intentional, and self-directed.

The claim is political and architectural: building a vault and inviting an agent into it is an act of tipping the scales toward emerging idea graphs while we still can. The grassroots community activity of building personal knowledge systems is counter-cultural precisely because the dominant mode is pure extraction.

The user's image: "farming yourself rather than being an oil derrick." Personal KM is sustainable cultivation; surveillance capitalism is extractive at scale and depth.
