A prompting method for exposing epistemic framing: ask an LLM to identify what a representational mode, metaphor, or argument highlights (foregrounds) and what it hides (suppresses or marginalizes). Named after a formulation derived from a conversation about *Metaphors We Live By* (Lakoff & Johnson).

The technique rests on the claim that every framing is a contract — it decides what counts as evidence, what's visible, and what's peripheral. Making the contract explicit is the analytic move. Works across metaphors ("argument is war"), LLM outputs on contested topics, data representations, and narrative structures. Can be applied reflexively to an LLM's own response.

Distinct from hallucination detection (this addresses structural bias, not factual error) and from bias auditing (more generative — surfaces assumptions of any framing, including good ones).
