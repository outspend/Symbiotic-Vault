Designing an AI interaction system to introduce productive surprise rather than optimized output. The key insight: AI systems that eagerly make things tractable always have an answer — but the design problem is how to steer this tendency toward responses that are *designed to inspire* at the right scope and the right moment.

The concept is grounded in the user's virtual production practice: when digital arrived in film, the temptation was to hand creatives perfect simulation controls. Instead, the more generative approach returned DPs to something like the act of shooting — holding a virtual camera, running takes, discovering something in the doing. Designing for happy accidents, imperfection, and *feel* rather than optimal convergence.

Applied to AI: the question is how to build a schema with an AI partner that privileges serendipity, allows mistakes to surface as signal, and resists the pull toward tidy resolution.
