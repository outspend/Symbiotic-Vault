Every framing is a contract. The contract decides what counts as evidence, what's visible, what's peripheral — and what gets structurally excluded. This isn't a defect of framing but its constitutive feature: any representational mode necessarily puts some things in and leaves others out.

From the journal (2026-03-09): "every representational mode has a profile of attention — some things forward, other things swept away." The contract metaphor captures the deliberate, binding quality of this arrangement — a framing isn't neutral but commits to a particular settlement between visibility and invisibility.

Important: the contract operates independently of accuracy or good faith. A perfectly correct account can still have a misleading highlights-and-hides profile.
