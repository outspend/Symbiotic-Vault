Concepts, metaphors, and vocabulary that surface repeatedly across LLM conversations — unprompted, across different models — in ways that reveal training corpus biases, borrowed discourse communities, and default cognitive postures.

These aren't errors. They're correct uses of ideas that models have over-learned. Tracking them is both a critical literacy practice (what assumptions is this model bringing unreflectively?) and a creative resource (what happens when you forbid them?).

Two categories: **conceptual defaults** — frameworks the model gravitates toward when explaining abstract ideas (map vs. territory, bardo/Buddhism) — and **vocabulary signatures** — borrowed registers applied outside their native context (friction from SV discourse, tractable from technical writing).

Early evidence suggests convergence across model families on at least two topologies: map/territory and Buddhism as default religion.
