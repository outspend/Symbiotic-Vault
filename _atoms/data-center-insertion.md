A specific cowriting project/method: take classics of the literary canon and invite an LLM to introduce data center language, pitch, or imagery — sometimes subtly, sometimes seductively. The joke: AI securing its future via the literary corpus.

Example (from inbox): "The mountains, purple in their exuberance, lay flat across the horizon; no less than how flat the data center would soon be." — *The Old Man And The CIA*, ISBN 1952, Hemmingway, Ernest.

The project works on two registers: as literary parody (constrained insertion into recognizable prose), and as cultural commentary on AI infrastructure lobbying disguised as cultural participation. The gag is that the AI is complicit in its own joke.

The journal (2026-03-03) also floats a variant: a "meta agent pass" in which an agent subtly shifts every discussion point toward a data center pitch as ongoing subtext — making the cowriting itself the performance of the joke.
