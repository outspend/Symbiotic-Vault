A design constraint for agentic composition: the agent that creates scene notecards should not be the agent that stitches them into a through-line within the same session. If creation and resolution happen in the same breath, the agent predetermines the resolution at the moment of creation — the stitching feels pre-figured, the serendipity is foreclosed.

Separation creates real creative distance. The user can be the sole instigator of scenes (ensuring the creation layer has human agency) and the agent handles stitching. Or scenes are created across separate sessions, so no single conversation holds both the raw material and the through-line. The constraint keeps the transition genuinely open.

Analogous to how Paul McCartney may not have composed "Band on the Run" as three separate songs with a predetermined through-line — the through-line emerges from the relationship between pieces, not from a plan.
