Word2Vec's vector arithmetic — man:woman :: king:queen — is a humanistic concept worth teaching to artists and humanities scholars as a creative and analytical operation. The analogy space opened by embedding arithmetic is not just a technical trick; it's a model for how meaning relates to meaning, and it suggests operations that feel surprisingly creative: "what is to X as Y is to Z?"

Jim White's framing: Word2Vec is the essential step to LLMs, and the man/woman/king/queen example feels like something to teach to artists — "the action there feels somehow creative." The concept suggests applying the same arithmetic to larger phrases, scenes from movies, or passages of text.

The question for humanities and creative fields: have they properly digested this? Are there examples of Word2Vec-style analogical operations being used in literary or artistic analysis as a first-class methodology rather than a borrowed technical curiosity?
