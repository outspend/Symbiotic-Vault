The writing domain develops and discovers practices through use — experimenting with prompting, constraint play, framing, coauthorship. The teaching domain structures those discoveries into communicable, teachable form. These two domains are not separate projects but a single continuous one: the vault is the connective tissue.

From LLM Best Practices: "The writing domain develops and discovers practices through use... The teaching domain structures those discoveries into things communicable to others. This note is the connective tissue."

The vault as personalized learning system is the clearest instance of this synthesis: writing about a system and building it are the same project. Neither domain completes the project without the other.

Implication for the methods collection: the collection isn't prescriptive (rules) but documentary (practices worth trying and examining). The difference matters for teaching.
