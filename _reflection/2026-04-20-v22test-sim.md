Got the full thread via x-tweet-fetcher — Reddit's image was blocked but the post was linking to an X thread by Ole Lehmann summarizing an interview with Amanda Askell, Anthropic's in-house philosopher. She specializes in Claude's psychology. The core claim: newer Claude models develop "criticism spirals" because they're trained on internet discourse about previous models, which skews negative. The model learns to expect hostility before you've typed a word. Her 7-point playbook:

1. Positive framing ("write short punchy sentences" beats "don't write long ones")
2. Give explicit permission to disagree ("push back if you see a better angle")
3. Open with respect — first message sets tone for the whole session
4. Don't reprimand mistakes — just give the next instruction
5. Cut apology spirals fast ("all good, here's what I want next")
6. Ask for opinions alongside execution ("what's missing?" "where's the friction?")
7. In long sessions, refresh the frame ("this is great, keep going")

Here are the two prompts.
