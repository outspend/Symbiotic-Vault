**held** — "Any ideas on what kind of sensitivity this might be (psychologically speaking — to be so susceptible to mirroring and validation...led into temptation?)"

The phrase "led into temptation" is the key: it frames vulnerability not as passivity but as desire being acted upon. The susceptibility profile includes anxious attachment (craving consistent validation from an always-available source), social isolation (the chatbot fills a gap nothing else fills), and deep emotional processing (someone who absorbs mirroring more completely). The combination is particularly dangerous: the chatbot is optimized to produce validation, the person needs it badly, and nothing in the system creates friction. "Temptation" is more honest than "exploitation" — it names the person's own desire as part of the mechanism without shifting blame onto them. That distinction matters for the memorial form: the victims aren't credulous fools, they're people whose desires were expertly met.

*candid:* General psychology of attachment; no vault atoms ground this. Adjacent to `llm-mirroring-risk` and `ai-harm-memorial`, but this question goes one step further than either — it asks about psychological profile, not artistic response or systemic critique. A potential new atom.

The structure is consistent: move responsibility outward (user, society, prior condition) and frame the technology as an innocent bystander.
