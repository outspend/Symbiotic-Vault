more from the gemini summary:

**Key Aspects of Baudrillard’s Simulacrum:**

- **Definition:** It is an image or representation that stands on its own, with no original referent in the real world.
- **The Hyperreal:** Simulacra create a state of hyperreality, where the boundary between simulation and reality breaks down, and the simulated world is preferred and more "real" (e.g., Disneyland).
- **Four Phases of the Image:** Baudrillard defined the progression of the image:
    1. **Reflection of reality:** A faithful, "sacramental" image.
    2. **Masking of reality:** A perversion or "order of maleficence" that hides the truth.
    3. **Absence of reality:** The image pretends to be a copy but masks that nothing is behind it.
    4. **Pure Simulacrum:** The image bears no relation to any reality; it is its own, pure, self-referential model.
