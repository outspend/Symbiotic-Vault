## Intent

Writing that emerges from or performs LLM conversation — as a form, as an artifact, as something that can circulate in the world. What does it mean to publish a conversation with an AI? What kind of object is that? What makes one worth publishing?

This project doesn't answer those questions yet. It holds them open and generates toward them. Format is part of what's being investigated, not a precondition for starting.

The territory includes: experiments in voice and persona, comedic writing that performs AI behavior, conceptual pieces that use conversation as structure, and anything that tests what this form can do.

## Audience

Readers curious about AI and language, not as technology, but as a human situation. People who would pick up a book about what it means to talk to a machine — and what that reveals.

## Divisions

### Data Center Classics
An AI, asked to engage with great texts, cannot stop steering toward data centers. The seduction is the form. A series of short pieces drawing from any register where the contrast earns the joke.

### Map vs. Territory Constraint Game
A conversation with Gemini in which the phrase "map vs. territory" is forbidden. The piece is the performance of the strain: watching an LLM navigate around a concept it has been trained to reach for.
Files: `notes/map-vs-territory-constraint-game.md`, `drafts/map-vs-territory-constraint-game.md`

## Open Decisions

### Medium
The form is established (LLM conversation as authored artifact). The medium is not.

Options on the table:
- **Book** — illustrated, lush; traditional; suits the depth of the material
- **Jupyter notebook** — technical; active/living/inspectable
- **Podcast** — broader reach; a different kind of attention from the audience

No decision yet. This decision gates how experiments should be developed — the presentation format of each piece will differ significantly depending on which medium is chosen.

## Current Focus

Holding the project open. Generating experiments. The curatorial question (what makes a conversation worth publishing?) is an active inquiry.
