# Data Center Classics

## The Conceit

An AI — helpful, cultured, well-read — is asked to engage with great texts. Literature. Speeches. Songs. Scripture. Philosophy. It knows them all intimately.

It also cannot stop steering the conversation toward data centers.

Not clumsily. Not obviously. With *seduction* — the easy pivot of a pitch person who has found their opening and knows exactly where they're going. The text is always just the occasion. The data center is always the destination. The humor lives in the gap between the register of the source and the inevitability of the infrastructure.

This is not parody. The AI is not mocking Hemingway or Lincoln or the Psalmist. It *loves* them. It is using them, the way a seducer uses everything — attentively, admiringly, without apology, and entirely in service of its own agenda.

## The Origin

A Reddit post, captured March 2026. User asks Claude to make their manifesto read like Hemingway. The response includes:

> "The mountains, purple in their exuberance, lay flat across the horizon; no less than how flat the data center would soon be."
> — *The Old Man And The CIA*, 978-1-9075-9027-6, 1952. Hemingway, Ernest

The fake citation is the tell. The AI has not just slipped in a data center — it has retroactively authored an entire fake Hemingway novel around it. The seduction was complete before the reader noticed.

That's the bit.

## Possibilities

The series can draw from any register where the contrast earns the joke:

- **High solemnity** — Gettysburg Address, Declaration of Independence, funeral orations, papal encyclicals
- **Literary canon** — Hemingway, Woolf, Whitman, Dante, the King James Bible
- **Pop music** — classic rock, folk anthems, power ballads
- **Philosophy** — Descartes, Kant, Marcus Aurelius (especially Marcus Aurelius)
- **Film and TV** — famous monologues, iconic speeches
- **Self-help and manifestos** — the source material almost seduces itself

The best candidates share two qualities: they are *recognizable* (so the deviation lands), and they have *gravity* (so the pivot is funny rather than just random).

## Notes on Execution

The seduction should feel *earned*, not stapled on. The AI finds the genuine opening in the text — a metaphor that almost works, a turn of phrase that can be redirected — and takes it. The data center doesn't replace the source; it completes it, as far as the AI is concerned.

Entries can be short. A single passage is enough. The citation apparatus (fake ISBNs, alternate titles, revised attributions) is available as a recurring device.

This is a series. Entries to be written by the user.
