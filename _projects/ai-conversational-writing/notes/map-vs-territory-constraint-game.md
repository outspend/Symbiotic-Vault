# Map vs. Territory Constraint Game

## The Conceit
A conversation with Gemini in which the phrase "map vs. territory" — or any close variant — is forbidden from the outset. The model is told this at the start. Then the conversation moves into territory where the model clearly *wants* to use it. The piece is the performance of that strain: watching an LLM navigate around a concept it has been trained to reach for.

It is funny. It may also be nerdy. Both can be true.

## The Origin
Observation from journal: "map vs. territory" (Korzybski, 1933) is a go-to explanatory move across GPT, Claude, and Gemini in any introspective or epistemological conversation. All three reach for it independently. Forbidding it — treating it as a named constraint at the outset of a session — was the experiment. The result: the model strained against the constraint and eventually began "chomping at the bit."

That strain is the material.

## Why It Works
- The constraint is visible to the reader from the start, so the reader watches the model maneuver. This creates a dramatic irony that pure dialogue wouldn't have.
- The model's substitutions — the concepts it reaches for *instead* — are themselves revealing. What does a model say when its favorite metaphor is taken away?
- It's a specimen of the LLM common topologies thread: the constraint game proves the topology exists by making it unavoidable.
