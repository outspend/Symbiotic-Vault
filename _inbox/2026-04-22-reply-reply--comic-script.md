STYLE:

- rough caption boxes with hand-lettered text

- Slightly awkward, funny, messy human energy

- Avoid cinematic page design; use a denser comix page structure

- MIKE: young Michael Scroggins, white man in his 20s, slim, long brown 1960s hair, suede jacket, watchful, curious, slightly self-conscious

- CHARLES MANSON: slight, dark-eyed, magnetic, still, cross-legged when playing guitar

- BILLY HARPER: bearded, open smile, light hair and beard, sunny / pastoral in vibe

  

LAYOUT:

A single-page comic, dense and readable, arranged in 16 panels in a rough 3 / 3 / 3 / 4 / 3 structure.

  

IMPORTANT TEXT RULES:

- Use only the panel captions and dialogue below

- Keep captions readable and clean

  

PANEL 1:

Topanga Canyon hillside, late 1960s dusk. Dense chaparral, dirt trail. Mike crouches at the edge of scrub, looking across a creek toward lights and firelight glowing on the hillside below.

Caption: “I was living off of Highvale Trail in Topanga Canyon. One night I saw lights across the creek.”

  

PANEL 2:

Close profile of Mike moving through brush, curious and wary, listening in the dark.

Caption: “Following sounds in the dark. Same as when I followed my dog. That always ended in poison oak.”

  

PANEL 3:

Mike crests the rise. Below is a ramshackle hillside property. Firelight. Roughly twenty people gathered, mostly women, painting on large hanging sheets, laughing, moving, music drifting upward. Barely clothed - Beautiful and strange.

Caption: “It was a really great scene.”

  

PANEL 4:

Interior of the house. Bare floors covered in mattresses. Candles. A guitar against a wall. Communal warmth and mess.

No caption.

  

PANEL 5:

Charlie sits cross-legged playing guitar in an old 40s style. Young women clustered around him. His playing is the room’s center of gravity.

Caption: “Charlie played guitar. 40s style. His way of tuning the flock.”

  

PANEL 6:

Mike sits fully dressed among lightly clothed commune members. His suede jacket is buttoned and conspicuous, like quiet armor.

Caption: “I kept my suede jacket on. I didn’t hang it up. That way I didn’t have to give it away.”

  

PANEL 7:

Billy Harper, with light hair / beard and a broad open smile, trudges up a path carrying four large honey jugs on a yoke. Mike walks alongside him.

Caption: “My buddy Billy dug their philosophy. He was keeping bees, so he brought them four gallons of honey.”

  

PANEL 8:

Charlie receiving the honey jugs in a commune-to-commune exchange. Warm daylight. Slightly ramshackle setting.

Caption: “Turns out they were crashing the place. Chuck sweet-talked the landlady — gave her a car when she found out.”

  

PANEL 9:

Mike and Billy walking back down the hill at dusk, backs to viewer, Topanga Canyon below them.
