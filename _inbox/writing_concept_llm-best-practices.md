
# LLM Best Practices

## The Connection
The writing domain develops and discovers practices through use — experimenting with prompting, constraint play, framing, and coauthorship. The teaching domain structures those discoveries into things communicable to others. This note is the connective tissue: a growing collection of practices that belong to both.

## Domain A — Writing
Practices that improve how you work with LLMs as a writing partner. These emerge from real use — from sessions that worked, sessions that revealed something, and sessions that failed in interesting ways. They're personal and particular before they're general.

## Domain B — Teaching
A curriculum of epistemic and procedural skills for working with AI. Learnable, teachable, shareable. The practices developed in writing become the content of a course or guide — not as rules, but as methods worth trying and examining.

## The Synthesis
The same practices that make a better writer with AI are the ones worth teaching. Building the collection and building the curriculum are the same project. The vault itself is the proof of concept: personal AI as a personalized learning system, where discovery and documentation are continuous.
